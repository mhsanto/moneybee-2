Target Customer Groups

Our Personal Loans are designed to serve Malaysians from all walks of life. Whether you are a civil servant, a private employee, or someone who has struggled with credit before, our Hybrid AI + Human approach ensures you are matched with the most suitable lender — safely, quickly, and with long-term financial health in mind.

Civil Servants & GLC Employees

Many Malaysian banks and financial institutions provide exclusive personal loan packages for government employees, offering lower rates and longer repayment periods. With our platform, you can easily compare these options and choose what suits your budget best.

Lower-Income Earners (from RM1,500/month)

Traditional banks sometimes reject lower-income applicants. Our AI helps you find licensed lenders who accept monthly incomes starting at RM1,500, so you still have access to fair financing. Our Case Managers then guide you to borrow within your means.

Private Sector Employees & Professionals

If you are working in the private sector, you may need loans for education, home renovation, or emergency expenses. We help you compare multiple lenders instantly, so you don't waste time applying one by one.

Self-Employed & Gig Economy Workers

Freelancers, small business owners, and gig workers often face difficulties proving steady income. Our hybrid system combines AI-matching with human review, ensuring that even non-traditional income sources (like Grab drivers, online sellers, small traders) are considered fairly.

Borrowers with Lower Credit Scores

Having a few late payments in the past doesn't mean you don't deserve another chance. Our AI identifies lenders who are more flexible with risk, while our Case Managers provide advice to help you improve your repayment record and rebuild your credit health.

Islamic Financing Customers

Many Malaysians — Muslim and non-Muslim alike — prefer Shariah-compliant loans for their fairness and transparency. We match you with licensed Islamic banks offering personal financing that avoids riba (interest) and hidden charges, so you can borrow with confidence.

Eligibility

Eligibility for personal loans varies by lender, but here are the common requirements in Malaysia

Age:

  • 21 – 60 years old (some lenders accept up to 65).

Income:

  • Minimum monthly income from RM1,500 – RM2,000 depending on product type.

Employment:

  • Civil servants, private employees, self-employed, or gig workers.

Documents required:

  • MyKad (NRIC) – National ID
  • Latest 3 months’ salary slips
  • Latest 3–6 months’ bank statements
  • EPF statement or employment letter (if applicable)
